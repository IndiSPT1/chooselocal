Make Your Own Bamboo Plate

What You’ll Need:

  • Thick piece of mature bamboo culm (wide diameter)
  • Saw
  • Knife or chisel
  • Sandpaper
  • Food-safe natural oil (like coconut or linseed)

Steps:

1. Cut the Bamboo

Choose a section of thick bamboo (about 5–6 inches high). Saw a cross-section that includes both the wall and the base node. This base will act as the bottom of your plate.

2. Smooth and Shape

Use a chisel or knife to trim and shape the inside and outer edges. You can taper the sides or round off the rim depending on your style.

3. Sand it Down

Sand thoroughly to remove splinters and smooth the surface. Start with coarse and finish with fine sandpaper.

4. Oil and Cure

Rub on food-safe oil to seal and protect the bamboo. Let it sit and cure for a day or two.

5. Done!

Use it for serving dry foods (fruit, bread, snacks) or as a decorative item.
