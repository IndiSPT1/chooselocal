Bamboo Mat

Materials Needed:

  • Bamboo – Most common (especially in central Myanmar)
  • Knife or bamboo cutter
  • Fire or boiling pot (to soften materials)
  • Thread or thin bamboo fiber (for binding)
  • Optional: Natural dyes (turmeric, betel, charcoal, indigo)

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Bamboo
    • Select mature bamboo.
    • Split it into long, thin strips (about 1–2 cm wide).
    • Boil or heat over fire to soften and sterilize.
    • Let them dry in the sun.
    • Flatten with a mallet or press under weights.
  1. Leaves (Palm or Pandanus):
    • Harvest fresh leaves.
    • Remove midribs and split into long flat strands.
    • Boil or sun-dry to prevent mold.
    • Optional: Dye them with natural colors before weaving.
  1. Set Up the Base
    • Lay down several strips side by side — this forms the vertical “warp”.
    • Secure them with weights or clip to a wooden frame or floor.
  1. Waterproofing
    • Traditionally, natural lacquer or resin is applied to the fabric to make it water-resistant.
    • It also helps preserve the colors.
  1. Final Assembly
    • The canopy is carefully attached to the bamboo ribs.
    • The opening mechanism is tested to ensure smooth opening and closing.
    • Final decorative touches are added.
  1. Start Weaving
    • Weave the horizontal “weft” strips in and out, alternating over-under patterns.
    • Push each strip tightly to keep the mat firm.
    • Common patterns: checkerboard, herringbone, diamond motifs.
  1. Secure the Edges
    • Fold or double the edge strips for strength.
    • Bind with thread or more bamboo fiber.
    • Some mats are stitched with fabric or wrapped in rattan for durability.
  1. Finishing Touches
    • Trim excess fibers.
    • Optionally, rub with coconut oil or natural lacquer for shine and preservation.
    • Let the mat air-dry for a day or two before use.
