Bamboo Pen

Material You'll Need:

  • A bamboo stick (around 15–20 cm long, thumb-thickness)
  • A small saw or knife (for cutting and shaping)
  • Sandpaper
  • A sharp blade or chisel (to carve the nib)
  • Ink (India ink or calligraphy ink works great)
  • Optional: drill and refill ink cartridge if making a refillable pen



Steps to make Bamboo Dip Pen(for drawing/writing)

  1. Choose the Right Bamboo
    • Dry bamboo is best — not green/fresh (it can crack).
    • Choose a straight, smooth piece without splits.
  1. Cut the Bamboo
    • Cut the length of the pen (15–20 cm is ideal).
    • Sand the edges smooth.
  1. Make the Nib (the writing tip)
    • On one end, cut a slanted edge, like a quill or chisel.
    • Then, use a blade to split the center of the nib about 1–2 cm. This helps hold and release ink.
    • Sharpen the tip depending on the kind of strokes you want — flat for calligraphy, pointy for drawing.
  1. Smooth Everything
    • Sand the nib and handle so it feels nice in your hand.
    • Optional: lightly burnish or oil the surface for a more polished look.
  1. Dip and Write
    • Dip the nib in ink, shake off the excess, and start writing or drawing!
    • Clean with water after each use.
