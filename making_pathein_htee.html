Pathein Htee

What you'll need:

  • Bamboo – for the frame and handle
  • Cotton or silk fabric – for the umbrella canopy
  • Lacquer or natural dyes – for waterproofing and coloring
  • Wood – for the central rod and turning parts
  • Thread, nails, glue – for assembly
  • Paints and brushes – for decoration

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Frame Construction (Bamboo Work).
    • Select fine bamboo and split it into thin, equal-sized ribs.
    • Shape them carefully and connect them at the center using a small wooden hub..
    • A small bamboo ring helps the ribs to expand and collapse.
    • The handle is made from carved wood or strong bamboo.
  1. Making the Canopy
    • Fabric (usually cotton or silk) is cut into a circular shape.
    • The edges are sewn, and a hole is made in the center to fit the frame.
    • The fabric is then dyed or painted, often with vibrant patterns or floral designs.
  1. Decorating
    • Traditional designs include floral patterns, peacocks, and Burmese motifs.
    • Artisans hand-paint the designs using brushes.
    • Gold or metallic paint is often used for elegance.
  1. Waterproofing
    • Traditionally, natural lacquer or resin is applied to the fabric to make it water-resistant.
    • It also helps preserve the colors.
  1. Final Assembly
    • The canopy is carefully attached to the bamboo ribs.
    • The opening mechanism is tested to ensure smooth opening and closing.
    • Final decorative touches are added.
