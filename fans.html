Make Your Own Bamboo Fan

Materials Needed

  • Bamboo sticks (approximately 11 inches long and Â½ inch wide)
  • Decorative paper or fabric (optional, for added design)
  • Glue
  • Scissors
  • Push pin
  • 2-inch head pin
  • Needle-nose pliers
  • Pencil
  • Paintbrush (for applying glue)
  • Tassel or decorative button (optional)

Steps

Prepare the Bamboo Sticks

Trim the bamboo sticks to the desired length (about 11 inches). Ensure they are smooth and free from splinters.

Create Holes in the Sticks

Measure about 1 inch from one end of each stick and use a push pin to pierce a hole through the center. This will serve as the pivot point for the fan.

Assemble the Fan Frame

Stack the sticks together, aligning the holes. Insert the 2-inch head pin through the holes to bind them. Use needle-nose pliers to twist the end of the pin, securing the sticks while allowing them to pivot.

Arrange the Sticks

Spread the sticks out evenly in a fan shape on a flat surface. Ensure the spacing between each stick is uniform.

Attach the Decorative Covering

Place the decorative paper or fabric over the arranged sticks. Use a pencil to trace the outline of the fan shape. Cut out the traced shape and apply glue to the back. Carefully adhere it to the sticks, pressing firmly to ensure it sticks well.

Finish the Fan

Once the glue has dried, trim any excess material around the edges. Optionally, attach a tassel or decorative button at the pivot point for added flair.
